The Weekend

Our wedding will take place on Sunday 23rd of August. We have our venue, Knockerdown Cottages, from the evening of Friday the 21st until the morning of Monday the 24th of August. We'd love to invite you to celebrate with us throughout the weekend. You're welcome to join from Friday evening or on Saturday if you prefer.

About Knockerdown

We're lucky to have exclusive use of Knockerdown holiday village from Friday evening until Monday morning.

The site includes an indoor swimming pool, table tennis and pool table, a children's play area, and lots of lovely space to relax. There is also a reservoir nearby and lots of scenic walks in the area.

Jewish Wedding FAQs

Will it be a Jewish wedding?

Although technically not a Jewish wedding, we will have elements of Jewish religion and culture throughout the day

Do I need to do or wear anything different?

Absolutely not. Please wear whatever you normally would to a wedding. Our Jewish guests may wear kippot (small hats worn in prayer) and prayer shawls called tallitot, but nobody is obliged to do so.

Will the food be kosher?

Our catering won't be provided by a kosher caterer, but we will ensure there is a vegetarian option at all times. There are also self-catering facilities in the cottages if you would feel more comfortable cooking for yourselves.

Can I bring non-kosher food for my own cottage?

You're welcome to eat however you like in your own cottage.

Why is the wedding on Sunday?

Traditionally, Jewish people don't get married between sunset on Friday and sunset on Saturday as this is Shabbat (the day of rest)
